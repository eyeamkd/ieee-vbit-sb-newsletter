IEEE - Advancing Technology for Humanity

IEEE (Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers) is an internationally renowned, non-profitable professional organization. It is the world's largest association of technical professionals across the globe dedicated to technology for the benefit of humanity. IEEE and its members inspire a global community to innovate for a better tomorrow through its highly-cited publications, conferences, technology standards, and professional and educational activities. IEEE is the trusted “voice” for engineering, computing and technology information around the globe.


There are more than 420,000 IEEE members in more than 160 countries. IEEE publishes a third of the world’s technical literature in electrical engineering, computer science and electronics and is a leading developer of international standards that underpin many of today's telecommunications, information technology and power generation products and services.


IEEE, an association dedicated to advancing innovation and technological excellence for the benefit of humanity, is the world’s largest technical professional society. It is designed to serve professionals involved in all aspects of the electrical, electronic, and computing fields and related areas of science and technology that underlie modern civilization.

IEEE has a dual complementary regional and technical structure with organizational units based on geography and technical focus. It manages a separate organizational unit (IEEE-USA) which recommends policies and implements programs specifically intended to benefit the members, the profession, and the public in the United States.

Opportunities don’t happen. You’ll have to create them. It has been truly said that designing is a journey of discovering oneself. Or rather, one’s self.
I believe that every design begins with a story and the story of every designer, innately being unique.
It is a plethora of shades constricting within the Red, Green and Blue color model. A journey with an equal tinge of texture, contrast, and saturation.
I am D. Bhavani Sai and I’ve always had an extreme love for creativity, right from a very tender age. From drawing to playing with Lego, making clay models to doing creative activities, these were always a matter of great fun to me and delving deep into the world of creating something new, I consider it a great deal of satisfaction.
My journey of transformation from a basic designer to the design lead of IEEE YESIST 12 event is a treasure trove of experiences filled with many sleepless nights, finite rejections and infinite patience.

Besides, I had never quit anything in my life, I always saw things through. There were situations throughout this journey which have played an important role in my artistic transformation. And the most important one, Rejection! It is well versed that rejection puts you out of your comfort zone which is usually when you are at your best and that mistakes are a stepping stone to success. Those were my heydays when I was a part of the Graphic Designing portfolio of the Organizing team of Avishkar 2K17. It was a situation where I had to design 72 cover pictures and all of them were rejected. It was that one moment where I felt I do not fit for this job and Graphic Designing is not my cup of tea. I WANTED TO QUIT.
And that is when my team came into the play. It is rightly quoted that – “Talent wins games but teamwork wins championships.” After tons of motivation from my team, I could brood the fact that the very petty yet the essential designing skills were in scarce. And that’s when I started to see graphic designing in a whole new perspective which brought me much closer to where I stand today.
Gaining the professional experience in designing through that event, I put my leg forth to work for sectional level events of IEEE where I increased my network and thus I received various opportunities to work for national level IEEE events. This journey of mine has made me both scholarly and culturally entrenched, ambitious but not pretentious, self-deprecating yet confident, forthright but unassuming, delightfully irreverent yet appropriately respectful - a complex and whole human being.  And I can say with absolute certainty that as a result of everything I mentioned above I have been able to reinvent myself into a more confident individual. Designing made me realize that it is simple making it even more complicated. All I can say is, don’t ever give up. Persistence and determination always pays off

Journey of 'The Harsens'

On the 8 th and 9 th September, 2018, three students from VBIT, Rashmitha Varma, Pujith C S and; Akshata Ravinder represented IEEE - VBIT SB at the Saturday Sunday 12-hours [SS12] event held in Sri Lanka. The newsletter team took their interview on what their experience was.
What exactly is SS12 and how did you guys get an opportunity to be a part of it?
SS12 is an IEEE Project Competition that is conducted on an international level for the IEEE members of the R10 region. Students who have IEEE membership receive mails regarding the various competitions and conferences being held by IEEE. We received information regarding the competition through one such mail.
How was your experience being able to witness something outside India?
We got to experience and understand the culture and traditions of the place and interact with the participants from all over the world, we tried local delicacies and had the chance to watch the traditional dance at one of the cultural events. It was a beautiful place, and the people were extremely kind. So all in all, it was a wonderful experience!
What was the most memorable moment(s) of your journey?
There are countless memories we made in Sri Lanka. But my most favourite memory will always be the night when we had our dinner near the beach. We could hear the voice of the ocean and the ambience was beautiful and tranquil. There was a hotel singer, we called him and requested to sing a song. He sang some Sri Lankan song. We all started giggling and smiling looking at each other gesturing that we didn't understand a single word. Then we went to our Hostel which was just half a kilometre from the beach. We went on the rooftop and saw the beach view. It was legendary! Few of the foreigners joined us in the pool and we made new friends. We didn't sleep that night, we made new friends and new memories.
Could you describe briefly any particular idea or tech which caught your eye?
One tech which completely blew our mind was the app TechVidya made by a team from Mumbai. Their idea was to integrate Augmented Reality (AR) into learning for children. Cards with chips embedded in them can be scanned with their app using a mobile phone, upon which an AR Object on the table is visible on the screen in real- time. Each card shows a different animal and their movements. This also happened to be the winning Project of the SS12 competition.
What would your word of inspiration be for all the juniors who are looking up to IEEE?
The outlook on IEEE in our college from students to management is stupendous. Since its inception in 2006 our alumni worked very hard to earn the reputation of IEEE-VBIT SB. With their persevering efforts and selfless dedication our student branch emerged with remarkable success with more than 12 IEEE appreciation and scholarship awards. My advice to juniors of our college is to look IEEE as a source of opportunities to learn, network and collaborate. It gives you an exposure to network with different people around our country. When I say networking that doesn't mean just taking contact numbers and saving. It's about building professional relationships and collaborate to do something innovative. IEEE-VBIT SB office bearers are always ready to help and encourage students to do innovations. Our number of volunteers maybe less, but our vision is large.

Competitve Programming

Competitive Programming is the sport of solving computational problems using Data Structures, Algorithms of Computer Science and also the implementation of various mathematical strategies in order to construct a computer program which gives desired output for wide range of inputs.  Every Problem in a Programming Competition consists of three parts which are

1.Problem Statement
  2.Constraints
3.Example

Problem Statements: Extensively describes the problem scenario for which a computer program has to be created.
  Constraints: Constraints specify the input range and the output range of the values given to the program 
Example: Describes an example input and the output obtained from that particular input.
A typical Competitive Programming problem looks somewhat like this  -
Problem Statement: Given a number find the reverse of its binary representation and print the sum of the number and the decimal number formed by reversing its binary representation  
Input Constraints :  1 to 1000
Input : 3
Output: 15


Why should a CS Student practice Competitive Programming?
Strangely, Competitive Programming is the best way to improve Data Structures and Algorithm skills, for any Computer Science individual irrespective of whether he’s in his Bachelor's or Master's or working professional or even a research scholar.


What is the use of improving DS and Algorithm skills, you ask?
Well, consider any top-notch software application that you are acquainted with, for instance Google, Facebook, Instagram etc.  They work seamlessly because of the extensive use of Data Structures and Algorithms in the Backend of the application.

“A Computer Science Engineer is not a Computer Science Engineer unless and until he is well versed with DS and Algo”
Having a strong hold on DS and Algo gives you the luxury to try out new ways of implementing the regular stuff in your applications. The reason why Google Search is so efficient is due to the DS and Algo implementation they use (Page Rank Algorithm). Any Major software company before employing/recruiting any Software engineer first tests his/her knowledge on  DS and Algo.

How should I start about with Competitive Programming? The best time to start competitive programming is in your 2nd semester. It doesn’t matter even if it’s too late. If you’re ready to practice hard then by the end of engineering you can surely be an expert at it.  Widely popular websites to practice competitive programming are: SPOJ spoj.com is a problem archive, consisting of a wide variety of problems from beginner to an expert level. SPOJ is the first place any programmer would begin his carrier with.
CodeChef is the only popular Indian Competitive programming site maintained by Direct-i which conducts monthly competitions
CodeForces is globally famous competitive programming site which conducts regular weekly competitions.
HackerRank has topic wise problems which also evaluates your global rank with every problem you solve.

Smartphone 101

In this era of rapidly developing technology, a smartphone is more than a comfort. It is a necessity for everyone these days, and thus choosing the perfect phone for you might seem like a tiresome task with so many companies coming up with a latest model every week. So let us look at the basic things you need to look for, while buying a smartphone.
There are 5 parameters based on which we can judge a smartphone:
1. Performance
2. Battery
3. Camera
4. Display
5. Build

Performance: When we talk about smartphone performance, the first and the foremost thing to look out for is the processor. Qualcomm’s Snapdragon release quality processors in many variants. We have 400 series for low-budget phones, 600 series for mid-range and 800 series for flagship models. Currently, Snapdragon 845 is the latest and the fastest chip that they offer. Contrary to the popular belief, more RAM does not always imply a faster speed. A simple example would be the comparison of RAM between Xiaomi Redmi Note 6 Pro which offers 6GB RAM which is smoothly outperformed by Google Pixel 3 XL with just 4GB RAM. Thus, we must also take the amount of optimization offered by the device into consideration. Choosing a phone which has the pure stock Android experience like the Pixel series or near-stock experience like the OxygenOS by OnePlus without any custom UI or bloatware is highly recommended if high performance is your criteria. My pick for the best performing Smartphone would be the OnePlus 6T. With 8GB RAM and Snapdragon 845, it’s a beast of a phone in terms of performance.

Battery: Smartphones nowadays consist of a Li-ion or a Li-Po battery whose measuring parameter is mAh which is milli Ampere hour. A standard 3000mAh battery may last for 4-5 hours on a continuous heavy usage. Although, higher number indicates a better battery life, it is not the only judging criteria. OS optimization also plays a major role. This includes how intelligently a phone is handling multi-tasking, background processes and memory. The phone with the best battery life according to me would be the Huawei Mate 20 Pro. It comes with a whooping 4200mAh battery which can last you upto 8 hours of continuous usage very easily.

Camera: Smartphones nowadays are coming with a dual camera and even a triple camera which may make you wonder if they’re going for an overkill. The Smartphone camera is judged based on various factors apart from the number of MegaPixels of the camera. The second camera can offer a Telephoto lens which is designed to get you as close to a distant subject as possible. With their higher focal length, telephoto lenses let you zoom in generally between 2-2.3x of the frame without compromising on the image quality. The image processor used also helps in enhancing the image quality to a great extent. Google Pixel 3 XL, which is my pick for the best smartphone camera, offers a stupendous image quality with just a single camera.

Display: The display of a smartphone has come a long way from an LCD Panels to the latest AMOLED and OLED displays. PPI which stands for Pixels Per Inch is a parameter which helps in determining the quality of the display. Higher the pixel density, crispier is the display quality. Samsung has been a top producer of smartphone displays with even the latest iPhones using them. My pick for the best smartphone display would go to theSamsung Galaxy Note 9whose Infinity Display never fails to charm me.

Build: Coming to the last parameter, we have the build quality of the phone. A lot of phones lately have been using metal and/or glass for making their phone. Having a glass back enables wireless charging whereas a metal back helps in improving the sustainability of the smartphone. Apart from the quality, the design and the aesthetic factor must also be considered. My choice would be the Oppo Find X which gave a unique solution to the notch problem and at the same time maintaining the build.

Conclusion: When it comes to choosing an ideal smartphone, it all comes down to your usage patterns and which parameters you prioritize. I hope this article helps you in finding that one unique phone designed for you.
